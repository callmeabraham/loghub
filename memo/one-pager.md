# LogHub — One-Pager: First Product & First 30 Days

## What we'd build first as the real product

This MVP proves the core mechanic: a grounded, source-cited AI Intake Assistant that can qualify a real move and draft a structured request. The **real v1 product** takes that mechanic and makes it something a moving company can actually run their front desk on:

1. **Bring-your-own-knowledge onboarding.** Instead of us hand-writing a firm's knowledge base, a firm uploads or links its own pricing sheet, FAQ, and AGB/terms, and the system builds its grounded knowledge base automatically. This is what makes the product scale past one hand-built demo firm.
2. **Real channels, not just a website chat widget.** The intake assistant needs to live where customers actually reach out: a WhatsApp Business number and a phone line at minimum (the Communication Hub block), not only an embedded web chat.
3. **A human-in-the-loop review dashboard.** Every Draft Move Request the assistant produces lands in a simple dashboard for office staff to review, edit, and approve before it becomes a real booking — directly addressing the "owners are wary of AI handling customer communication unsupervised" risk from the investment memo. The AI drafts; a human still confirms, at least at first.
4. **Instrumentation for the moat hypothesis.** Every firm's deployment tracks lead-to-booking conversion and how often the assistant needed correction, over time — this is the data that tells us whether "compounding company memory" is real or not.

## First 30 days: three moves

1. **Recruit 3–5 AMÖ-member moving companies as free design partners**, via the trade association's member network. Goal: real firm documents (pricing, FAQ, AGB) ingested into real knowledge bases — not demo content — and a first look at the actual questions their customers ask, which will differ from our guesses.

2. **Ship bring-your-own-knowledge onboarding + the human-review dashboard**, and put the intake assistant live for at least 2 design partners with real customer traffic (website chat and/or WhatsApp), not just internal testing.

3. **Start the moat and pricing test.** Instrument conversion and correction-rate tracking per firm from day one, and open the pricing conversation with each design partner before their free period ends — real willingness to pay at €300–600/month is the signal that tells us whether to keep building, not just whether the demo impressed anyone.
