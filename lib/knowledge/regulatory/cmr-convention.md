## What the CMR Convention covers

The CMR Convention ("Convention on the Contract for the International Carriage of Goods by Road") is the uniform legal framework for cross-border road freight between more than 55 countries, including all EU member states. It applies automatically whenever goods are carried by road for reward across at least one national border, regardless of what the parties write in their own contract. It governs the consignment note (CMR waybill), carrier liability, and claims deadlines — it does not apply to purely domestic moves within one country, which instead fall under national law (e.g. German HGB, see the HGB Frachtvertrag source).

## Carrier liability (Article 17)

The carrier is liable for total or partial loss of, or damage to, the goods between taking them over and delivery, and for delay in delivery — including loss/damage caused by employees or subcontractors used for the carriage. Liability is fault-based but with a reversed burden of proof: the carrier must prove one of the listed exemptions applies, the claimant does not have to prove fault.

**Exemptions from liability** (carrier is *not* liable if the loss/damage results from):
- circumstances the carrier could not avoid and the consequences of which it was unable to prevent
- use of open, unsheeted vehicles when this was expressly agreed
- lack of or defective packing (where visible defects were not noted)
- handling, loading, stowing, or unloading done by the sender or consignee themselves
- the natural condition/nature of certain goods (e.g. shrinkage, spoilage)
- insufficient or imprecise marking of packages
- carriage of live animals

## Liability limits (Article 23)

Compensation for loss/damage is capped at **8.33 SDR (Special Drawing Rights) per kilogram of gross weight** of the goods lost or damaged, unless the value of the goods or a special interest in delivery was declared and a surcharge paid. This cap does **not** apply if the loss was caused by the carrier's intent or gross negligence — in that case, liability is unlimited under Article 29.

## Documentation

The consignment note (CMR waybill) is not itself a condition of the contract's validity, but it is the primary evidence of the terms of carriage and of the condition of goods at handover — practically essential for any liability claim.

## Claims and time limits

Any reservation about visible damage should be noted on the consignment note at delivery; the general limitation period for CMR claims is **one year** (extended to three years in cases of wilful misconduct).
