## Cabotage — the "3-in-7" rule

Cabotage is when a haulier registered in one EU country carries out domestic transport operations inside another EU country, after delivering an international load there. Under Regulation (EC) 1072/2009 (as amended by Regulation (EU) 2020/1055, in force since 21 February 2022):

- After fully unloading an incoming international shipment, a vehicle may perform **up to 3 cabotage operations within 7 calendar days**.
- Operations can be spread across **up to 3 different member states** (other than the one where the international load was unloaded), but **only 1 operation per additional member state**, and it must be completed within 3 days of unladen entry into that state.
- After the last cabotage operation in a given member state, the same vehicle is subject to a **4-day cooling-off period** before it may perform cabotage there again.

This matters directly for cross-border household moves and freight: a moving company running a truck from Germany into France, then wanting to pick up a second domestic job within France before returning, is doing cabotage and must stay inside these limits — violations carry fines and can trigger vehicle immobilization.

## EORI number

An **EORI number** (Economic Operators Registration and Identification) is required for any customs operation — import, export, or transit — involving non-EU countries. It is issued free of charge by the customs/tax authority in the country where the business is established, and once obtained is valid for all customs dealings across the EU.

## T1 transit document

A **T1** is an electronic customs document (tracked in the EU's NCTS system) used to move goods that have not yet cleared EU customs ("non-Union goods") through EU territory without import duties/VAT becoming due at every border crossing. Duties are suspended during transit and only become payable once the goods are cleared at their final destination. A T1 cannot be opened without a valid EORI number. For road transport, a T1 must generally be discharged (goods presented at the destination customs office) within **5 days**.

## Practical relevance for LogHub-type moves

Fully intra-EU household moves (e.g. Germany → France) generally do **not** require customs formalities, since goods move freely within the EU single market. T1/EORI questions become relevant mainly for: (a) moves involving non-EU countries (e.g. Switzerland, UK, Norway), or (b) commercial freight cargo rather than personal household goods. A firm should flag any move touching a non-EU border for a customs-document check before booking.
