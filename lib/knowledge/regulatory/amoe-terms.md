## What AMÖ is

The AMÖ (Bundesverband Möbelspedition und Logistik e.V.) is the German trade association for moving/furniture-transport companies. Member firms operate under a standardized moving contract and standard liability terms (Allgemeine Beförderungsbedingungen), giving customers a predictable liability framework across different moving companies rather than each firm writing its own terms from scratch.

## Liability limit for household goods

Under the AMÖ standard terms (which reflect **§ 451e HGB**), a moving company's basic liability for loss or damage to household goods is capped at **EUR 620 per cubic metre** of the moving volume, unless a higher liability was separately agreed and, typically, an additional premium paid. This is a statutory-anchored default, not an arbitrary company policy — it applies to AMÖ member firms operating under these terms.

## Ancillary services (non-freight work)

Moving crew members are generally **not authorized** to carry out electrical, gas-fitting, wall-mounting/drilling, or other installation work unless this was explicitly agreed in advance. For contractually agreed services that fall outside the core freight contract (e.g. disassembly/reassembly of complex furniture, appliance connection), liability is capped separately at **EUR 50,000 per claim**, distinct from the per-cubic-metre freight liability cap.

## Duty of care

The moving company is obliged to perform the move with the greatest care, in the customer's interest, in exchange for the agreed price — including reasonable protection of goods during loading, transport, and unloading.

## Dispute resolution

Customers who are private consumers can bring disputes to the **AMÖ mediation office (Schlichtungsstelle)**, which operates under its own procedural rules and is **free of charge** for consumers — an alternative to going straight to court.

## Practical relevance

When a customer asks "what happens if something breaks," the answer generally has two layers: (1) the statutory/AMÖ default liability (EUR 620/m³) that applies automatically, and (2) any additional transport insurance the customer purchased for higher-value or higher-risk items (see the company's own insurance & special-item coverage policy for specifics like piano transport).
