## Where we operate

- **Regular corridors** (our own fleet, weekly departures): Germany ↔ Netherlands, Belgium, France, Austria, Switzerland.
- **On-request EU destinations** (via partner carriers, 5-7 working days lead time to schedule): all other EU/EEA countries.
- **Switzerland**: EU customs rules don't apply since Switzerland is outside the EU customs union — see the customs note below.
- **Outside EU/EEA/Switzerland**: not currently served directly; we can refer to a partner international freight forwarder.

## What's different from a domestic move

- **Longer lead time**: book at least 3 weeks ahead for regular-corridor countries, 5+ weeks for on-request destinations.
- **CMR consignment note required**: every cross-border road move is covered by the CMR Convention rather than German HGB domestic freight law — this changes the liability basis and cap (see the CMR regulatory source). Practically: liability follows the CMR per-kilogram cap (8.33 SDR/kg) rather than the domestic EUR 620/m³ figure, unless higher declared-value coverage was purchased.
- **Customs for non-EU legs**: for intra-EU moves (e.g. Germany → France), there are no customs formalities — goods move freely within the EU single market. For moves touching Switzerland or any non-EU country, a customs declaration and (if applicable) a T1 transit document are required — see the EU cabotage & customs regulatory source. We handle this paperwork for our own regular-corridor routes; for other non-EU destinations via partner carriers, customs handling is arranged with the partner and quoted separately.
- **Access/parking rules abroad**: local parking-permit rules vary by country and city; we coordinate this with our destination-country partners rather than the customer arranging it themselves.

## Pricing note

International quotes are always custom (volume, distance, corridor, and whether it's a full-load or shared/part-load shipment all matter) — there is no fixed rate table like the domestic one. Expect a quote within 2-3 business days of a fully-qualified request (addresses, volume, target date, any special items).

## Cabotage note (business customers / freight, not household moves)

If a business customer needs multiple domestic pickups/deliveries within a single non-German EU country using the same vehicle, this falls under EU cabotage rules (maximum 3 operations within 7 days per country, see the regulatory cabotage source) — relevant mainly for commercial freight bookings, not typical household moves.
